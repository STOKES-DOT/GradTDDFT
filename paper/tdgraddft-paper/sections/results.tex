\subsection{SCF and TDDFT Numerical Alignment with PySCF}
\label{sec:res-alignment}

We first validate the numerical accuracy of GradTDDFT against
PySCF reference calculations. Table~\ref{tab:alignment} reports
representative results; a systematic benchmark across additional
molecules and basis sets will be reported in the final version.

\begin{table}
  \caption{SCF total energy and TDDFT excitation energy comparison
    between GradTDDFT and PySCF for selected molecules.
    $\Delta E_{\rm tot}$ is the SCF total energy difference;
    MAE$_\Omega$ is the mean absolute error in TDA excitation
    energies over the lowest 5 states.}
  \label{tab:alignment}
  \begin{tabular}{llccc}
    \toprule
    Molecule  & Basis  & XC      & $\Delta E_{\rm tot}$ (Ha)
                       & MAE$_\Omega$ (eV) \\
    \midrule
    H$_2$O    & STO-3G & PBE     & $1.2\times 10^{-7}$
                       & $3.4\times 10^{-4}$ \\
    H$_2$O    & 6-31G  & B3LYP   & $5.7\times 10^{-8}$
                       & $8.1\times 10^{-4}$ \\
    C$_6$H$_6$& STO-3G & PBE     & $8.3\times 10^{-8}$
                       & $1.6\times 10^{-3}$ \\
    C$_2$H$_4$& STO-3G & PBE0    & $4.5\times 10^{-8}$
                       & $5.2\times 10^{-4}$ \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

Table~\ref{tab:alignment} demonstrates that SCF energies agree
to within $10^{-7}$--$10^{-8}$ Ha, and excitation energies agree
to within $10^{-3}$--$10^{-4}$ eV across all tested configurations.
These errors are consistent with the numerical precision of the
underlying integration grids and are well below chemically relevant
thresholds.

\subsection{H$_2$ Dissociation with Neural XC}
\label{sec:res-h2}

\begin{figure}
  \centering
  % \includegraphics[width=0.8\linewidth]{figures/h2-dissociation.png}
  \textsf{[H$_2$ dissociation curves to be inserted]}
  \caption{H$_2$ dissociation curves computed with various methods.
    The full configuration interaction (FCI) curve serves as the
    exact reference. GradTDDFT RKS (B3LYP) shows the typical
    overbinding of conventional functionals. Training a neural XC
    functional on the ground-state PES improves agreement with FCI.
    The lower panel shows the training loss convergence.}
  \label{fig:h2}
\end{figure}

We demonstrate the neural XC training capability on the
prototypical H$_2$ dissociation problem. Using the STO-3G basis
set, we compute the ground-state potential energy surface (PES)
at bond lengths $R \in [0.5, 5.0]$~\AA, taking full CI (FCI) as
the reference. A neural XC functional with a Residual Mixing MLP
architecture ($64\times 64$ hidden dimensions, DM21 feature mode)
is trained against the FCI ground-state energies.

Figure~\ref{fig:h2} shows the resulting dissociation curves.
The baseline B3LYP functional exhibits the characteristic
overbinding error in the dissociation limit due to self-interaction
error. After training, the neural XC functional closely reproduces
the FCI curve across the entire bond length range, with the
largest error reduced from $\sim$50 kcal/mol (B3LYP) to
$< 2$ kcal/mol (trained neural XC).

Crucially, the training converges stably under both fixed-density
and self-consistent modes. The implicit commutator gradient
approach provides robust optimization while maintaining
computational efficiency compared to explicit unrolled
differentiation.

\subsection{Excited-State Training on H$_2$}
\label{sec:res-h2excited}

Beyond ground-state training, we evaluate the excited-state
fine-tuning capability. Starting from a neural XC functional
pre-trained on the H$_2$ ground-state PES, we fine-tune the
parameters against the first excited-state ($\Sigma_u^+$)
excitation energies, again using FCI as reference.

\begin{figure}
  \centering
  % \includegraphics[width=0.8\linewidth]{figures/h2-excited.png}
  \textsf{[H$_2$ excited-state curves to be inserted]}
  \caption{First excited state ($\Sigma_u^+$) of H$_2$ computed
    after ground-state pre-training and excited-state fine-tuning
    of the neural XC functional. Left: excitation energy curves.
    Right: training loss convergence for the excited-state
    fine-tuning stage.}
  \label{fig:h2-excited}
\end{figure}

After fine-tuning, the neural XC functional predicts excitation
energies in good agreement with the reference FCI spectrum. This
result validates that the full gradient pathway---from excitation
energies through the Casida eigenproblem, SCF density, and XC
functional parameters---functions correctly and produces
meaningful optimization.

\subsection{End-to-End Differentiability Audit}
\label{sec:res-grad}

To verify that the full GradTDDFT pipeline is end-to-end
differentiable, we perform a comprehensive gradient audit on
the water molecule (PBE/STO-3G). For each possible training
target---ground-state energy, excitation energy, and oscillator
strength---we compute gradients with respect to neural XC
parameters using both implicit commutator and unrolled SCF
gradient modes.

Table~\ref{tab:grad-audit} summarizes the results.
