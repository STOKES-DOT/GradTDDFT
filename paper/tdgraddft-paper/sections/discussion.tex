\section{Discussion and Outlook}
\label{sec:discussion}

We have presented GradTDDFT, a JAX-based differentiable toolkit
for TDDFT with neural exchange-correlation functionals. The
framework provides a PySCF-compatible API with full end-to-end
differentiability, enabling the training of neural XC functionals
directly against excited-state observables.

Several directions for future development are identified:

\textbf{Strict PT2 response kernel.} The current neural XC
functional includes a local PT2 surrogate head whose orbital
response is treated in the frozen-field approximation.
A stricter treatment would compute the full orbital-rotation
Hessian of the PT2 energy, enabling a doubles-dressed
singles response that more rigorously captures dynamical
correlation effects in excited states.

\textbf{Full-JAX integral engine.} While the current
implementation relies on PySCF for integral computation, a
JAX-native integral pathway has been prototyped and validated for
angular momenta up to $l \leq 3$. Substantial optimization work
remains before this pathway can replace PySCF's compiled C
integrals in production workflows.

\textbf{Unrestricted differentiable SCF.} Extending the
self-consistent differentiable SCF to general unrestricted
configurations would enable training against properties of
open-shell systems and charged states, and support strict
Janak-based training objectives for range-separated hybrids.

\textbf{Larger basis sets and systems.} The current numerical
validation has focused on compact basis sets (STO-3G, 6-31G).
Scaling to larger basis sets (def2-TZVP and beyond) and more
complex molecular systems remains an open challenge requiring
algorithmic improvements in the J/K construction and gradient
propagation strategies.

\textbf{Broader training objectives.} Future versions may
support training against additional experimental observables
such as excited-state gradients, nonadiabatic couplings, and
two-photon absorption cross sections, taking advantage of the
full differentiability of the framework.

In summary, GradTDDFT provides a versatile platform for
developing and training next-generation exchange-correlation
functionals optimized for excited-state accuracy. By combining
the robustness of the PySCF integral engine with the
differentiability of JAX, it bridges the gap between traditional
quantum chemistry software and modern machine learning workflows.
