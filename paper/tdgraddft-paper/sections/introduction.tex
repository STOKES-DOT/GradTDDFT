\section{Introduction}

Time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) within the
linear-response formalism of Casida\cite{Casida1995} is the
most widely used method for computing excited-state properties
of molecules. Its accuracy is fundamentally limited by the
quality of the exchange-correlation (XC) functional. Conventional
semi-local functionals fail systematically for charge-transfer
excitations, Rydberg states, and double excitations---motivating
the development of more accurate functional forms.

Neural network XC functionals, trained on accurate reference data,
have recently achieved significant improvements in ground-state
DFT accuracy.\cite{DM21,GLOBE} However, the question of whether
neural XC functionals can be systematically optimized for
\emph{excited-state} properties remains largely unexplored.
Answering it requires a TDDFT framework that is fully
differentiable: capable of propagating gradients from excited-state
observables (excitation energies, oscillator strengths) back through
the Casida eigensolver, the SCF cycle, and the XC functional
parameters.

Existing quantum chemistry packages such as PySCF,\cite{PySCF}
Q-Chem,\cite{QChem} and Gaussian\cite{Gaussian} are implemented
in C/C++ or Fortran and are not designed for automatic
differentiation. The emergence of differentiable programming
frameworks, particularly JAX,\cite{JAX} has enabled a new generation
of tools with automatic differentiation as a design principle.
Notably, GradDFT\cite{GradDFT} provides a differentiable
ground-state DFT framework, but it does not support TDDFT
excited-state calculations or excited-state loss propagation.

To address this gap, we present GradTDDFT, a JAX-based
differentiable toolkit for TDDFT whose central motivation is to
\emph{enable direct training of neural XC functionals against
excited-state properties}. GradTDDFT features:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Excited-state neural XC training.} Training utilities
    that directly optimize neural XC functionals against excited-state
    observables---excitation energies and oscillator strengths---with
    support for ground-state pre-training followed by excited-state
    fine-tuning.

  \item \textbf{Strict $f_{\rm xc}$ kernel via JAX autodiff.} The
    exchange-correlation kernel is computed as the exact Hessian of
    the neural XC local energy density with respect to reduced density
    variables, using JAX automatic differentiation. This eliminates
    finite-difference noise and correctly handles the cross-derivatives
    between learned coefficients and density variables that are
    essential for hybrid and neural hybrid functionals.

  \item \textbf{PySCF-compatible differentiable pipeline.} A library
    with a PySCF-style API whose SCF $\rightarrow$ TDDFT $\rightarrow$
    observable computational graph is fully differentiable through JAX,
    enabling gradient flow from any observable back to XC parameters.
\end{enumerate}

The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. Section~2
describes the theoretical framework: differentiable KS-SCF,
linear-response TDDFT, the strict $f_{\rm xc}$ kernel, and the
neural XC architecture and training paradigm. Section~3 details
the software implementation and PySCF integration. Section~4
presents numerical validation---SCF/TDDFT alignment against PySCF,
H$_2$ dissociation with neural XC training, and end-to-end
differentiability audits. Section~5 discusses current limitations
and future directions.
