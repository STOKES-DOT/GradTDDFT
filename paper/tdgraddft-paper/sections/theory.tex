\section{Theory and Methods}
\label{sec:theory}

The main theoretical construction follows the organization used in
GradDFT-style differentiable functional work\cite{GradDFT}: first
define the energy functional and its parameterization, then specify
the response equations used to obtain observables, and finally state
how losses are differentiated through the SCF and response maps.
Detailed descriptor definitions, response-kernel contractions, and
neural hybrid channel definitions are deferred to the Supporting
Information.

\subsection{DFT and Neural Hybrid XC Functional}
\label{sec:theory-dft}

For a molecular system with density $\rho$, the Kohn--Sham energy is
written as
\begin{equation}
  E_\theta[\rho]
  =
  T_s[\rho]
  + E_{\rm ne}[\rho]
  + J[\rho]
  + E_{\rm xc}^{\theta}[\rho]
  + E_{\rm nn},
  \label{eq:dft-energy}
\end{equation}
where $T_s$ is the noninteracting kinetic energy, $E_{\rm ne}$ is the
electron--nuclear attraction, $J$ is the Hartree energy, $E_{\rm nn}$
is the nuclear repulsion, and $\theta$ denotes the parameters of the
learned exchange-correlation (XC) model. The corresponding
generalized eigenvalue problem in an AO basis is
\begin{equation}
  \mathbf{F}_\theta[\mathbf{D}]\mathbf{C}
  =
  \mathbf{S}\mathbf{C}\boldsymbol{\epsilon},
  \qquad
  \mathbf{F}_\theta
  =
  \mathbf{h}
  + \mathbf{J}[\mathbf{D}]
  + \mathbf{V}_{\rm xc}^{\theta}[\mathbf{D}]
  + \mathbf{V}_{\rm hyb}^{\theta}[\mathbf{D}],
  \label{eq:ks-fock}
\end{equation}
where $\mathbf{D}$ is the density matrix and
$\mathbf{V}_{\rm hyb}^{\theta}$ collects nonlocal hybrid
contributions.

The neural functional is expressed as a coefficient-basis hybrid
functional with three physically motivated channel groups:
local/semi-local XC channels, a local exact-exchange HF channel, and
a local MP2/PT2 second-order correlation channel. This mirrors the
doubly local double-hybrid idea, where local mixing functions control
both exact exchange and PT2 correlation in real space.\cite{KovacsDLDH2026}
On a quadrature grid,
\begin{equation}
  E_{\rm xc}^{\theta}
  =
  \sum_g w_g e_{\rm xc}^{\theta}(g),
  \qquad
  e_{\rm xc}^{\theta}(g)
  =
  \sum_{m=1}^{M}
  c_m^{\theta}(\mathbf{z}_g)e_m^{\rm sl}(g)
  + c_x^{\theta}(\mathbf{z}_g)h_g
  + c_2^{\theta}(\mathbf{z}_g)p_g .
  \label{eq:neural-hybrid-functional}
\end{equation}
Here $\mathbf{z}_g$ is a local descriptor vector, $c_m^\theta$,
$c_x^\theta$, and $c_2^\theta$ are learned mixing fields, and
$e_m^{\rm sl}$ are local or semi-local functional energy densities.
A general semi-local channel has the form
\begin{equation}
  e_m^{\rm sl}(\mathbf{r})
  =
  \rho(\mathbf{r})\,
  \varepsilon_m^{\rm sl}
  \!\left(
    \rho_\sigma,
    \nabla\rho_\sigma\!\cdot\!\nabla\rho_{\sigma'},
    \tau_\sigma
  \right),
  \label{eq:semilocal-channel}
\end{equation}
with LDA, GGA, and meta-GGA channels obtained by selecting the
corresponding subset of density variables. For example, a
spin-unpolarized LDA exchange channel and a GGA exchange channel may
be written as
\begin{equation}
  e_x^{\rm LDA}(\mathbf{r})
  =
  -C_x\rho(\mathbf{r})^{4/3},
  \qquad
  e_x^{\rm GGA}(\mathbf{r})
  =
  e_x^{\rm LDA}(\mathbf{r})F_x(s),
  \qquad
  s=
  \frac{|\nabla\rho(\mathbf{r})|}
       {2(3\pi^2)^{1/3}\rho(\mathbf{r})^{4/3}},
  \label{eq:lda-gga-channel-example}
\end{equation}
where $C_x=\frac{3}{4}(3/\pi)^{1/3}$ and $F_x$ is the
functional-specific GGA enhancement factor. Through jax-xc
\cite{JAXXC}, different conventional semi-local functional families
can be inserted into this basis without changing the SCF or TDDFT
equations.

The HF and MP2/PT2 ingredients are exposed to the neural model only
through local channel densities,
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
  E_x^{\rm HF}
  &=
  \int h(\mathbf{r})\,d\mathbf{r}
  \simeq
  \sum_g w_g h_g,
  \\
  E_c^{(2)}
  &=
  \int p(\mathbf{r})\,d\mathbf{r}
  \simeq
  \sum_g w_g p_g .
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:local-hf-pt2-energy-main}
\end{equation}
The corresponding grid descriptors are
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
  h_g
  &=
  -\frac{1}{2}
  \sum_{\sigma}\sum_{ij}^{\rm occ}
  n_{i\sigma}n_{j\sigma}
  \phi_{i\sigma}(\mathbf{r}_g)
  \phi_{j\sigma}(\mathbf{r}_g)
  v_{ij\sigma}^{x}(\mathbf{r}_g),
  \\
  p_g
  &=
  \frac{1}{4}
  \sum_{ijab}
  t_{ij}^{ab}\eta_{ij}^{ab}(\mathbf{r}_g).
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:local-hf-pt2-descriptors-main}
\end{equation}
Thus $c_x^\theta(\mathbf{z}_g)$ and
$c_2^\theta(\mathbf{z}_g)$ are pointwise mixing fields for the local
exact-exchange and second-order correlation channels, rather than
global scalar fractions. The orbital-pair potential
$v_{ij\sigma}^{x}$, the second-order amplitude $t_{ij}^{ab}$, and
the real-space pair projection $\eta_{ij}^{ab}$ are defined in the
Supporting Information.
Constant coefficients in Eq.~\ref{eq:neural-hybrid-functional}
recover a conventional hybrid or double-hybrid form, while learned
pointwise coefficients define the neural hybrid functional used in
GradTDDFT.

\subsection{Linear-Response TDDFT, TDA, and Casida Equations}
\label{sec:tddft}

After SCF convergence, excitation energies are obtained from
linear-response TDDFT. For occupied orbitals $i,j$ and virtual
orbitals $a,b$, the full Casida equation is
\begin{equation}
  \begin{pmatrix}
    \mathbf{A} & \mathbf{B} \\
    \mathbf{B} & \mathbf{A}
  \end{pmatrix}
  \begin{pmatrix}
    \mathbf{X}_I \\
    \mathbf{Y}_I
  \end{pmatrix}
  =
  \Omega_I
  \begin{pmatrix}
    \mathbf{1} & \mathbf{0} \\
    \mathbf{0} & -\mathbf{1}
  \end{pmatrix}
  \begin{pmatrix}
    \mathbf{X}_I \\
    \mathbf{Y}_I
  \end{pmatrix},
  \label{eq:casida-main}
\end{equation}
where $\Omega_I$ is the excitation energy. The response matrices have
the schematic form
\begin{align}
  A_{ia,jb}
  &=
  (\epsilon_a-\epsilon_i)\delta_{ij}\delta_{ab}
  + K_{ia,jb},
  \\
  B_{ia,jb}
  &=
  K_{ia,bj},
  \label{eq:ab-main}
\end{align}
where $K$ contains Coulomb, XC-kernel, and hybrid-response
contributions. For the semi-local part,
\begin{equation}
  f_{\rm xc}^{\theta}(\mathbf{r},\mathbf{r}')
  =
  \frac{\delta^2 E_{\rm xc}^{\theta}}
       {\delta\rho(\mathbf{r})\delta\rho(\mathbf{r}')},
  \label{eq:fxc-main}
\end{equation}
so the potential used in SCF and the local kernel used in TDDFT are
derivatives of the same energy-density model. HF and MP2/PT2
channels are assigned distinct response constructions in the
excited-state calculations. For the HF channel, the pointwise
coefficient field is reduced to a density-weighted scalar exchange
fraction,
\begin{equation}
  a_x^\theta
  =
  \frac{\sum_g w_g \rho_g c_x^\theta(\mathbf{z}_g)}
       {\sum_g w_g \rho_g},
  \label{eq:effective-hf-fraction-main}
\end{equation}
and the TDDFT matrices use the conventional hybrid exact-exchange
response
\begin{equation}
  K_{ia,jb}^{x,A}=-a_x^\theta(ij|ab),
  \qquad
  K_{ia,jb}^{x,B}=-a_x^\theta(ib|aj).
  \label{eq:hf-response-main}
\end{equation}
The local HF descriptor $h_g$ can still condition the learned
coefficients, but it does not introduce a separate orbital-response
kernel for the projected local HF field.

For the PT2 channel, GradTDDFT follows the double-hybrid excited-state
logic of solving the singles response first and then adding a
root-specific second-order doubles correction.\cite{HeadGordonCISD1994,GrimmeNeeseDoubleHybrid2007}
The learned PT2 coefficient field defines
\begin{equation}
  a_c^\theta
  =
  \frac{\sum_g w_g \rho_g c_2^\theta(\mathbf{z}_g)}
       {\sum_g w_g \rho_g},
  \qquad 0\le a_c^\theta\le 1,
  \label{eq:effective-pt2-fraction-main}
\end{equation}
and the final excitation energy is evaluated as
\begin{equation}
  \Omega_I
  =
  \Omega_I^{\rm LR}
  + a_c^\theta \Delta_I^{\rm CIS(D)} .
  \label{eq:pt2-posthoc-main}
\end{equation}
Here $\Omega_I^{\rm LR}$ is the TDA or Casida root obtained from the
semilocal and HF response matrices, and
$\Delta_I^{\rm CIS(D)}$ is the state-specific second-order correction
computed from the singles amplitudes and the occupied--virtual
energy denominators. Thus the PT2 channel
enters excited-state energies as a doubles correction rather than as
an additional local response tensor in the TDDFT matrix.

The Tamm--Dancoff form sets $\mathbf{B}=0$, yielding the Hermitian
eigenvalue problem
\begin{equation}
  \mathbf{A}\mathbf{X}_I=\Omega_I\mathbf{X}_I .
  \label{eq:tda-main}
\end{equation}
Oscillator strengths are evaluated from the transition dipole,
\begin{equation}
  \boldsymbol{\mu}_I
  =
  \sum_{ia}
  (X_{ia}^{I}+Y_{ia}^{I})\,\boldsymbol{\mu}_{ia},
  \qquad
  f_I
  =
  \frac{2}{3}\Omega_I|\boldsymbol{\mu}_I|^2,
  \label{eq:osc-main}
\end{equation}
with $Y_{ia}^{I}=0$ in the TDA case.

\subsection{Differentiable Training Objectives}
\label{sec:training}

GradTDDFT treats the fixed molecular data as
$\mathcal{I}$ and writes the SCF solution as a fixed point
\begin{equation}
  \mathbf{D}_\theta^\ast
  =
  \Phi_\theta(\mathbf{D}_\theta^\ast;\mathcal{I}),
  \qquad
  \mathbf{R}_\theta(\mathbf{D})
  =
  \mathbf{D}-\Phi_\theta(\mathbf{D};\mathcal{I}).
  \label{eq:scf-fixed-point-main}
\end{equation}
The observable map
$\mathcal{O}_\theta(\mathbf{D};\mathcal{I})$ includes ground-state
energies, TDDFT excitation energies, and oscillator strengths. A
general training loss is written
as
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{J}(\theta)
  =
  \lambda_{\rm GS}\mathcal{L}_{\rm GS}
  + \lambda_{\rm ES}\mathcal{L}_{\rm ES},
  \label{eq:loss-main}
\end{equation}
where the two terms denote ground-state and excited-state targets.
For a given training run in this work, one objective family is selected
at a time by setting either $\lambda_{\rm ES}=0$ or
$\lambda_{\rm GS}=0$. This notation therefore describes the two
separate protocols within one mathematical framework.

The same forward calculation supports three differentiation choices.
In fixed-density training, a reference density is held fixed:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{J}_{\rm fd}(\theta)
  =
  \ell\!\left(
    \mathcal{O}_\theta(\mathbf{D}^{\rm ref};\mathcal{I}),
    \mathbf{y}
  \right),
  \qquad
  \frac{d\mathbf{D}^{\rm ref}}{d\theta}=0.
  \label{eq:fd-main}
\end{equation}
In explicit self-consistent training, a finite SCF trajectory is
differentiated directly:
\begin{equation}
  \mathbf{D}_{n+1}=\Phi_\theta(\mathbf{D}_n;\mathcal{I}),
  \qquad
  \mathcal{J}_{\rm exp}(\theta)
  =
  \ell\!\left(
    \mathcal{O}_\theta(\mathbf{D}_{N};\mathcal{I}),
    \mathbf{y}
  \right).
  \label{eq:explicit-main}
\end{equation}
In implicit self-consistent training, the converged density satisfies
$\mathbf{R}_\theta(\mathbf{D}_\theta^\ast)=0$, and its parameter
derivative follows from
\begin{equation}
  \frac{d\mathbf{D}_\theta^\ast}{d\theta}
  =
  -
  \left(
    \frac{\partial\mathbf{R}_\theta}{\partial\mathbf{D}}
  \right)^{-1}
  \frac{\partial\mathbf{R}_\theta}{\partial\theta}.
  \label{eq:implicit-main}
\end{equation}
These equations define the differentiable routes available to the
model; the final training schedule can combine them with different
weights, targets, and response-kernel constructions.
